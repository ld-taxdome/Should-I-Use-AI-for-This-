Operations
Summarizing Last Quarter's Team Performance
Your manager has asked you to put together a summary of last quarter's team performance. You want to use an AI tool to help structure and write it. The summary will include individual team members' names, output metrics, and notes from one-to-one check-ins — including a mention that one team member is going through a difficult period personally.

Does this task include personal information about named individuals?
Names combined with performance data or personal context counts as sensitive personal data.

Does it include sensitive HR information (personal difficulties, health context)?
References to personal difficulties, health, or wellbeing — even without full detail — are sensitive.

Would an error or misrepresentation in this summary cause harm to a team member?
Performance summaries that reach management can affect someone's career, reputation, or wellbeing.
?
Does this task require empathy, judgment, or knowledge of the individuals involved?
Performance summaries often require nuance that AI cannot reliably provide.
?
Is this tool on your company's approved list for processing HR-related content?
HR data often has stricter data handling requirements than general content.
?
Would a team member feel uncomfortable knowing AI had processed information about them?
The comfort and trust of the people whose data is involved matters — not just the legal minimum.
🚫
Don't Use AI
This summary contains named individuals, sensitive HR data, and personal context that requires human judgment and care. AI should not process this content — the risk of misrepresentation, data exposure, and harm to team members is too high. Write this yourself, keep it factual, and handle any personal context with appropriate sensitivity and discretion.

Customer Success
Responding to a Customer Complaint About Billing
A customer has emailed to complain about an unexpected charge on their account. The charge was an error on your company's side. You want to use AI to help draft a response. You plan to include the customer's name, account number, and the specific amount of the incorrect charge in the AI prompt to get a personalized response.

Run through the checklist before deciding
?
Does the prompt include the customer's name and account number?
Names and account numbers are PII and sensitive financial identifiers.
?
Does it include a specific financial amount tied to the customer?
Billing amounts linked to named customers are confidential financial data.
?
Could you replace all identifying details with placeholders and still get a useful draft?
For example: "Help me draft an apology for an incorrect charge of $X to Customer A, Account #XXXX."
?
Does this complaint involve a company error that requires a genuine, accountable response?
Situations where your company is at fault need human accountability — not just a polished AI draft.
?
Will a human review and personalize the response before sending?
A complaint response should feel human, not templated — even if AI helped draft it.
?
Is the AI tool on your company's approved list?
Even with anonymized data, only use approved tools for customer-related tasks.
⚠️
Use with Caution
AI can help you draft this response — but only if you remove all identifying details first. Replace the customer's name, account number, and billing amount with placeholders before prompting. Once you have a draft, re-insert the correct details yourself, personalize the tone, and review carefully. The human sending this response must own the accountability — not the AI that helped write it.
